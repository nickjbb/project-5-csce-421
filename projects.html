    • 2024 TAMIDS Data Science Competition
    Last Spring, I collaborated on a machine learning project for the annual TAMIDS Data Science Competition. For this competition, we were tasked with creating a ML model to predict the potential rise of sea level rise along the New York Coast. For this project, I mainly worked on the data analysis, where I used R and Python libraries like Plotly and Matplotlib to understand and visualize the data. Finally, our group wrote a 10 page report of the data analytics and the ML algorithm to present to a board of experts. Our team were able to place 2nd in the competition with over 40 teams competing.
